  • Supplements unreliable piped water supply
  • Reduces dangerous over-extraction of groundwater (especially critical in Kathmandu Valley)
  • Provides water during long dry seasons when springs disappear
  • Helps control urban flooding by capturing monsoon runoff
  • Recharges shallow aquifers when designed with recharge structures
  • Offers good quality water — often drinkable after simple filtration
  • Supports kitchen gardens, livestock and small-scale irrigation
  • Low-cost climate adaptation measure for changing rainfall patterns

A typical 100 m² rooftop in Kathmandu can harvest 50,000–80,000 liters during one monsoon — enough to make a real difference for a family of 5–6 people.
