1. Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting

Most popular method in both urban and rural Nepal.

Roof → gutters → first-flush diverter → filter → storage tank (ferro-cement, plastic, underground).

Best for: Kathmandu Valley, mid-hills, individual houses, schools, offices.

2. Plastic-lined Pond / Surface Harvesting

Large UV-resistant plastic-lined ponds collect surface runoff for irrigation and livestock.

Best for: Mid-hills and mountain villages, community-level agriculture.

3. Recharge Pits & Trenches

Directs rooftop or surface water into pits/trenches to recharge groundwater.

Best for: Areas with depleting aquifers (Kathmandu Valley, Pokhara, urbanizing hills).

4. Ferro-cement Tanks & Jar Systems

Durable, low-cost storage promoted by ICIMOD and local NGOs.

Best for: Remote mountain areas, scattered households.
